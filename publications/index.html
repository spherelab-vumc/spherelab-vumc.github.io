Publications

1. Social Media and Personal Health Mentions

  • A Scalable Framework to Detect Personal Health Mentions on Twitter
    Journal of Medical Internet Research, 2015.
    A methodology for identifying health-related discussions on Twitter, enhancing understanding of public health conversations.

  • Detecting Personal Health Mentions from Social Media Using Supervised Machine Learning
    Personal Health Informatics, 2022.
    Explores machine learning techniques for identifying personal health discussions in social media, with implications for precision health.

  • The Power of the Patient Voice: Learning Indicators of Treatment Adherence From An Online Breast Cancer Forum
    Proceedings of the Eleventh International AAAI Conference on Web and Social Media, 2017.
    Analyzes patient discussions to extract adherence indicators, emphasizing the value of patient-generated content.

2. Health Data Analysis and Machine Learning

  • A Systematic Literature Review of Machine Learning in Online Personal Health Data
    Journal of the American Medical Informatics Association, 2019.
    Reviews existing literature on machine learning applications in personal health data, identifying gaps and future research directions.

  • Mining Medication Use Patterns from Clinical Notes for Breast Cancer Patients Through a Two-Stage Topic Modeling Approach
    AMIA Informatics Summit, 2022.
    Discusses a novel topic modeling approach to extract medication patterns from clinical notes, enhancing treatment understanding.

  • Optimizing Word Embeddings for Small Datasets: A Case Study on Patient Portal Messages from Breast Cancer Patients
    Scientific Reports, 2024.
    Investigates techniques to enhance word embeddings for health-related text analysis, particularly in small datasets.

3. Patient Experiences and Communication

  • Understanding Weight Loss via Online Discussions: Content Analysis of Reddit Posts Using Topic Modeling and Word Clustering Techniques
    Journal of Medical Internet Research, 2020.
    Analyzes weight loss discussions to uncover common themes and motivations in online forums.

  • The Therapy is Making Me Sick: How Online Portal Communications Between Breast Cancer Patients and Physicians Indicate Medication Discontinuation
    Journal of the American Medical Informatics Association, 2018.
    Examines communication patterns that signal medication discontinuation, highlighting the role of patient-provider interactions.

  • Learning When Communications Between Healthcare Providers Indicate Hormonal Therapy Medication Discontinuation
    AMIA Annual Symposium Proceedings, 2018.
    Focuses on identifying signals in provider communications that could predict medication adherence issues.

4. Ethical Considerations and Privacy

  • Using Game Theory to Thwart Multistage Privacy Intrusions When Sharing Data
    Science Advances, 2021.
    Proposes a game-theoretic framework for addressing privacy concerns in health data sharing.

  • When to Share Your Genome to an Online Community: A Game Theoretic Approach to Balance Privacy Risks and Familial Benefits
    Scientific Reports, 2022.
    Discusses the implications of sharing genetic information in online settings, balancing privacy with potential familial benefits.

5. Other Notable Publications

  • The Public Perception of the #GeneEditedBabies Event Across Multiple Social Media Platforms: Observational Study
    Journal of Medical Internet Research, 2022.
    Investigates public reactions to gene editing discussions on social media.

  • Learning From Peers: A Survey of Perception and Utilization of Online Peer Support Among Informal Dementia Caregivers
    arXiv preprint, 2023.
    Surveys informal caregivers to understand their use of online peer support and its implications for caregiving practices.
